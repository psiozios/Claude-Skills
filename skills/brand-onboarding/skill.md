# Brand Onboarding

## Overview
Packages brand language, tone, visual guidelines, and communication standards into a shareable format for contractors, agencies, and new team members. Creates a brand bible that ensures partner alignment with your brand identity.

## When to Use This Skill
- Hiring a new contractor or freelancer
- Onboarding a marketing agency
- Adding new team members
- Starting a collaboration that requires brand consistency

## Process

### Step 1: Compile Brand Assets

**Voice & Tone:**
- Brand personality (3-5 adjectives)
- Tone spectrum (when to be formal vs casual)
- Key phrases and taglines
- Words we use vs words we avoid
- Example paragraphs in our voice

**Visual Identity:**
- Logo files (all variants) and usage rules
- Color palette with hex/RGB values
- Typography (fonts, sizes, hierarchy)
- Photography/illustration style
- Icon style and usage

**Communication Standards:**
- Email signature format
- Social media guidelines
- Presentation template
- Document formatting rules
- Response time expectations

### Step 2: Create Onboarding Package

```markdown
# Brand Onboarding Guide: [Brand Name]

## Welcome
[Brief intro to who we are and what we value]

## Quick Reference
- Primary color: [hex]
- Heading font: [name]
- Body font: [name]
- Voice in 3 words: [adjectives]

## Dos and Don'ts
### Do:
- [specific positive examples]

### Don't:
- [specific negative examples with corrections]

## Assets
[Links to logo files, templates, font files]

## Examples
[3-5 examples of on-brand work]

## Contact
[Who to ask when unsure]
```

### Step 3: Distribute and Validate
- Share via accessible link (not email attachment)
- Ask partners to create a sample piece before starting real work
- Review the sample against the guide
- Iterate on the guide based on common misunderstandings

## Key Rules
- Keep it short enough to actually read (under 10 pages)
- Include examples of what NOT to do (not just positive examples)
- Provide downloadable assets, not just descriptions
- Update when brand evolves — stale guides cause inconsistency
- Include a "when in doubt" contact person
