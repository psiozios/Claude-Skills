# Email Conversion Tester

## Overview
Tests emails for conversion optimization issues before sending. Analyzes subject line strength, preview text, CTA clarity, value proposition, reading level, spam trigger words, and mobile rendering considerations. Provides a conversion score with specific improvements.

## When to Use This Skill
- Before sending any marketing or sales email
- When open rates or click rates are declining
- Testing a new email template
- Reviewing automated email sequences

## Process

### Audit Checklist

**1. Subject Line (Score /25)**
- Length: 30-50 characters ideal
- Personalization: includes name or relevant detail
- Curiosity or benefit: gives a reason to open
- Spam triggers: avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, "free", "act now"
- Preview text: complements (not repeats) the subject

**2. Body Content (Score /25)**
- Opening line: hooks within first sentence
- Value proposition: clear within first paragraph
- Reading level: grade 6-8 for most audiences
- Paragraph length: max 3 sentences
- Scanability: bold key phrases, use whitespace

**3. CTA (Score /25)**
- Visibility: stands out visually
- Clarity: specific action ("Get the guide" vs "Click here")
- Count: one primary CTA, one secondary max
- Placement: above the fold AND at the bottom
- Urgency: reason to act now (without being pushy)

**4. Technical (Score /25)**
- Mobile preview: content readable at 320px width
- Image alt text: present for all images
- Link functionality: all links valid
- Unsubscribe: visible and functional
- Load time: images optimized, no heavy embeds

### Output
- **Overall Score**: /100 with grade (A through F)
- **Top 3 Issues**: Most impactful improvements
- **Quick Wins**: Changes that take under 5 minutes
- **Rewritten Version**: Optimized email with changes highlighted

## Key Rules
- Always test from the reader's perspective, not the sender's
- One email, one goal — mixed CTAs kill conversion
- Subject line and preview text are 80% of the battle
- If the email wouldn't be useful without the CTA, it's just an ad
