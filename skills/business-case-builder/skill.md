# Business Case Builder

## Overview
Builds investment cases through a structured 6-step workflow — problem definition, cost analysis, benefit quantification, risk assessment, ROI calculation, and recommendation. Supports quick (1-page), standard (3-page), and comprehensive (full deck) output formats.

## When to Use This Skill
- Requesting budget for a new initiative
- Justifying a technology investment
- Proposing a new hire or team expansion
- Making a build vs buy decision

## Process

### Step 1: Problem Definition
- What problem are we solving?
- Who is affected and how severely?
- What happens if we do nothing? (cost of inaction)
- What's the urgency?

### Step 2: Solution Options
For each option (including "do nothing"):
- Brief description
- Implementation approach
- Timeline
- Resource requirements

### Step 3: Cost Analysis
Quantify all costs:
- **One-time costs**: Setup, migration, training, hardware
- **Recurring costs**: Licenses, maintenance, staffing, infrastructure
- **Hidden costs**: Opportunity cost, learning curve, integration effort
- **Timeline**: When costs are incurred

### Step 4: Benefit Quantification
Quantify all benefits:
- **Revenue impact**: New revenue, upsell, reduced churn
- **Cost savings**: Automation, efficiency, reduced errors
- **Risk reduction**: Compliance, security, business continuity
- **Intangible benefits**: Team morale, brand perception, speed

### Step 5: ROI Calculation
- **Payback period**: When cumulative benefits exceed costs
- **3-year ROI**: Total benefits minus total costs
- **NPV**: Net present value at appropriate discount rate
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Best/worst/expected scenarios

### Step 6: Recommendation
- Recommended option with clear rationale
- Key risks and mitigations
- Decision criteria and trade-offs
- Next steps and timeline

### Output Formats
- **Quick** (1 page): Executive summary, costs, benefits, recommendation
- **Standard** (3 pages): Full analysis with supporting detail
- **Comprehensive** (deck): Presentation-ready with appendix data

## Key Rules
- Always include the "do nothing" option as a baseline
- Quantify everything possible — vague benefits don't persuade
- Be conservative on benefits, generous on costs (builds credibility)
- Include sensitivity analysis — single-point estimates are misleading
- End with a clear recommendation and specific next step
