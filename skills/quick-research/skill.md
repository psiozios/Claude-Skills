# Quick Research

## Overview
Compresses research on any topic into a structured brief — executive summary, key findings, data points, source quality assessment, knowledge gaps, and recommended next steps. Designed for fast decision-making, not academic depth.

## When to Use This Skill
- Need to get up to speed on a topic quickly
- Preparing for a meeting or presentation
- Evaluating a market, technology, or trend
- Building initial context before deeper investigation

## Process

### Step 1: Define Scope
- What question are you trying to answer?
- What decisions will this inform?
- How deep do you need to go? (surface / moderate / thorough)
- Time constraint (15 min / 1 hour / half day)

### Step 2: Research
- Gather information from available sources
- Focus on authoritative and recent sources
- Look for data points, not just opinions
- Note conflicting information

### Step 3: Structure the Brief

```markdown
## Research Brief: [Topic]
**Date**: [date] | **Depth**: [surface/moderate/thorough]

### Executive Summary
[3-5 sentences answering the core question]

### Key Findings
1. [Finding with supporting evidence]
2. [Finding with supporting evidence]
3. [Finding with supporting evidence]

### Data Points
- [Statistic or fact + source]
- [Statistic or fact + source]

### Source Quality
- High confidence: [what's well-supported]
- Low confidence: [what's uncertain or conflicting]

### Knowledge Gaps
- [What we don't know and why it matters]
- [What would require deeper research]

### Recommended Next Steps
1. [Action based on findings]
2. [Further research if needed]
```

## Key Rules
- Answer the question in the first paragraph — don't bury the lead
- Distinguish facts from opinions from speculation
- Note source quality — a single blog post is not the same as peer-reviewed research
- Identify what you DON'T know (knowledge gaps are as valuable as findings)
- Keep it under 2 pages — if it's longer, it's not "quick" research
