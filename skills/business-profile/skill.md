# Business Profile

## Overview
Creates and maintains a central reference document for business positioning. Captures mission, value propositions, target segments, competitive advantages, brand voice, and key messaging. Serves as context input for all other marketing, sales, and communication skills.

## When to Use This Skill
- Setting up a new project or business
- Onboarding AI tools to understand your business
- When outputs across skills feel inconsistent
- Annually or when strategy shifts significantly

## Process

### Step 1: Core Identity
```markdown
# Business Profile: [Company Name]

## Mission
[One sentence: what you do and why it matters]

## Vision
[One sentence: where you're going]

## Value Propositions
1. [Primary]: [Benefit for customer]
2. [Secondary]: [Benefit for customer]
3. [Tertiary]: [Benefit for customer]
```

### Step 2: Audience
```markdown
## Target Segments
### Segment 1: [Name]
- Who: [Demographics/firmographics]
- Pain: [Primary problem]
- Goal: [Desired outcome]
- Language: [How they describe their problem]

### Segment 2: [Name]
[Same structure]
```

### Step 3: Positioning
```markdown
## Competitive Position
- Category: [Market you compete in]
- Differentiators: [What makes you unique]
- Competitors: [Top 3-5 and your advantage over each]
- Pricing position: [Premium / mid-market / value]

## Key Messages
- Elevator pitch (10 sec): [...]
- Elevator pitch (30 sec): [...]
- Tagline: [...]
```

### Step 4: Voice & Tone
```markdown
## Brand Voice
- Personality: [3-5 adjectives]
- Tone: [Formal/casual spectrum]
- We say: [preferred terms]
- We never say: [banned terms]
```

## Key Rules
- This document is the single source of truth for all business context
- Update when strategy changes, not on a fixed schedule
- Keep it under 2 pages — if it's too long, no one (human or AI) will use it
- Test by asking someone unfamiliar to read it and pitch your business back to you
