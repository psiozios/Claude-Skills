# Decision Council

## Overview
Runs multiple expert personas — financial, technical, customer, legal, and operations — to analyze a decision from different angles simultaneously. Synthesizes perspectives into a recommendation with dissenting views clearly noted.

## When to Use This Skill
- Making a high-stakes business decision
- When you need multiple perspectives but don't have the people
- Evaluating a proposal before presenting to leadership
- Breaking out of a single-perspective analysis

## Process

### Step 1: Frame the Decision
- What decision needs to be made?
- What are the options (minimum 2)?
- What constraints exist (budget, timeline, resources)?
- What's the decision deadline?

### Step 2: Convene the Council
Run each persona's analysis:

**Financial Advisor:**
- Cost-benefit analysis for each option
- Cash flow implications
- ROI projections
- Financial risk assessment

**Technical Expert:**
- Feasibility assessment
- Technical debt implications
- Scalability considerations
- Integration complexity

**Customer Advocate:**
- Impact on user experience
- Customer satisfaction implications
- Competitive positioning
- Churn risk

**Legal/Compliance:**
- Regulatory implications
- Contractual obligations
- Liability exposure
- Compliance requirements

**Operations Lead:**
- Implementation complexity
- Resource requirements
- Timeline feasibility
- Operational risk

### Step 3: Synthesis
```markdown
## Decision Council Report: [Decision]

### Consensus Points
[Where all personas agree]

### Divergent Views
[Where personas disagree, with reasoning]

### Recommendation
[The recommended option based on weighted perspectives]

### Key Risks
[Top 3 risks with mitigation strategies]

### Dissenting Opinion
[The strongest argument against the recommendation]

### Decision Criteria
[What should ultimately drive this decision]
```

## Key Rules
- Every persona must genuinely advocate for their perspective (no token opposition)
- Include at least one perspective that disagrees with the obvious choice
- Dissenting views are features, not bugs — document them clearly
- The synthesis should acknowledge trade-offs, not just pick a winner
- Flag if a decision is reversible vs irreversible (changes the risk calculation)
